About Turkey

Turkey is a country located at the crossroads of Europe and Asia, making it a unique bridge between two continents. Its capital city is Ankara, while Istanbul is the largest and most famous city. Turkey has a rich history that dates back thousands of years, having been home to many civilizations such as the Byzantine and Ottoman Empires. The official language is Turkish, and the currency is the Turkish Lira. Turkey is well known for its historical landmarks, beautiful landscapes, and cultural diversity.

Turkish Culture

Turkish culture is a mix of Eastern and Western traditions. Family values play an important role in daily life, and hospitality is a key part of Turkish society. Traditional clothing can still be seen during festivals and special occasions. Turkey celebrates many national and religious holidays, such as Republic Day and Ramadan Bayram. Turkish music, folk dances, and arts reflect the country’s rich heritage and long history.

Famous Turkish Food

Turkish Kebab

The most famous Turkish dish, made of grilled meat with special spices.

Manti

These are tiny handmade dumplings filled with spiced ground meat, served with a creamy garlic yogurt sauce and topped with melted butter and dried mint.

Turkish Pide

Often called "Turkish Pizza", it's a boat-shaped flatbread with various toppings.

Lahmacun

Thin and crispy Turkish pizza topped with minced meat, vegetables, and herbs.

Kumpir

A famous Turkish street food consisting of a giant baked potato mashed with butter and cheese, then filled with various colorful toppings.

Balik Ekmek

Sliced lamb topped with hot tomato sauce over pieces of bread and melted butter.

Simit

The most iconic Turkish street food, a circular bread encrusted with sesame seeds, known for its crunchy crust and soft interior, often enjoyed with tea and cheese.

Menemen

A popular Turkish breakfast dish made with eggs, tomatoes, green peppers, and spices.

Discover the Common Dishes Between Egyptian and Turkish Cuisines

Sütlaç(Rice Pudding)

A beloved creamy dessert in both Egypt and Turkey, made from rice, milk, and sugar. The Turkish version (Sütlaç) is often baked in clay bowls to create a delicious caramelized top.

Baklava

A rich, sweet dessert made of layers of filo pastry filled with chopped nuts.

Kunefe

A classic oriental dish of minced meat mixed with fresh parsley, onions, and a special blend of Mediterranean spices, perfectly grilled to a juicy finish.

​Lokma

Lokma is a Turkish fried sweet dough that is covered in a simple syrup.Served as a dessert

​Köfte

A classic oriental dish of minced meat mixed with fresh parsley, onions, and a special blend of Mediterranean spices, perfectly grilled to a juicy finish.

​Moussaka

A classic Mediterranean favorite shared between Egypt and Turkey, featuring sautéed eggplant and spiced ground meat, baked to perfection in a rich tomato sauce.

​Dolma

Tender grape leaves stuffed with a flavorful mixture of rice ,herbs and spices

​Mercimek Çorbası(Lentil Soup)

The most beloved Turkish starter, a creamy and soul-warming blend of red lentils and spices, served with a wedge of lemon and a drizzle of melted chili butter on top.
